Country-level safety & travel brief for solo travelers

Search a country once, then switch between a calm info view and a crisis-ready playbook.
This prototype is designed for both academic evaluation and real travel use.

We’ll fetch a live country profile (population, region, language) and combine it with a simple risk overview.
Then you can switch to Crisis Mode for step-by-step guidance if something goes wrong.
For the demo, try: France Japan Italy.

Once a country is selected, you'll see a simple visual breakdown of four dimensions: crime, political stability, health infrastructure, and natural hazard exposure.

Advisory source

When possible, we map to an official travel advisory level (e.g. Level 1–4). If live data is unavailable, we fall back to curated demo values for a few sample countries.

If something goes wrong

This mode turns the same country into a calm, practical checklist: what to watch out for, and who to contact.

Select a country above. We’ll show an overall risk label, the most relevant risk types for solo travelers, and a simple mindset reminder.

Emergency contacts

Police, ambulance, fire, and a placeholder for your home country’s embassy or consulate in this destination.

Crisis playbook

Common scenarios (lost passport, protests, sudden illness) with a step-by-step playbook. Below, you can also type a free-text question to get a short, AI-style answer powered by these rules.

Once a country is selected, we’ll surface 2–3 core scenarios with very concrete steps. This content is driven by your offline JSON and does not depend on network quality.

Ask a quick question

Example: “Public transport is on strike and I’m alone in the city center, what should I do first?”

Your answer will appear here as a short paragraph. In this prototype, it is generated on the client side using the offline safety JSON, but the UX is already shaped for a real AI backend.
